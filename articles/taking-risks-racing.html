Taking Risks in Business: Lessons from the Racing Track

Mustafa Tuncer — January 2017 — 4 min read
Nürburgring Nordschleife — "The Green Hell" · Photo: Unsplash
"If you no longer go for a gap that exists, you are no longer a racing driver." — Ayrton Senna

This quote perfectly summarizes not just the mentality of racing drivers, but also the mindset needed for business success — seizing opportunities and taking calculated risks.

Here's a perfect example that illustrates both the potential and the peril of this philosophy: Belgian driver Laurens Vanthoor's incident at Germany's legendary Nürburgring circuit.

The Green Hell

The Nordschleife section of the Nürburgring is called the "Green Hell" — a 20-kilometer narrow, dangerous track. The Dottinger Höhe straight is the most critical section where pilots reach speeds over 300 km/h.

Here, Vanthoor spotted a chance to overtake two slow cars in heavy traffic. Just as Senna advocated, he saw the gap and went for it. He began the maneuver with surgical precision but made slight contact with another car.

This minor contact, which would be insignificant under normal circumstances, created a domino effect at that high speed — the tire blew out, Vanthoor lost control, and the Audi crashed hard into the barriers. Fortunately, thanks to modern safety technologies, the pilot escaped unharmed, but the car was completely unusable and the team had to retire from the race.

The Business Parallel

This story perfectly mirrors business decision-making. Just like in racing, in business you must seize opportunities when you see them — but the key question is: are you aware of the risks you're taking?

The best leaders, like the best drivers, don't avoid risks — they take intelligent ones with full awareness of what's at stake.

What gaps are you considering in your business? How are you calculating the risk?

  • Are you aware of the risks you're taking?
  • Could this decision cause unexpected damage to your company?
  • If things go wrong, what's the impact on your team, your health, your mental well-being?
  • Are you taking calculated risks or chasing opportunities blindly?
